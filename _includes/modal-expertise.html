Marketing Analytics

Marketing data science can be used to better understand customers, measure marketing effectiveness, and increase marketing ROI. Our expert consultants have the skills to take your marketing efforts to the next level. We can help with:

  • Customer Journey Analysis: Using data science we can dive deep into understanding how your website vistors become customers and what touch-points and content are most critical to customer conversion.

  • Audience Segmentation: Understanding what groups exist within your customer-base can be critical to capitalizing on hidden trends and better serving your customers. We can use clustering techniques alongside supervised learning to group customers and make predictitions.

  • Data Management: We can help move and join data from Google Analytics, Google AdWords, Google BigQuery and internal offline data warehouses. Further, via webscraping we can collect data from public online sources.

  • Automated Analytics Reporting and Visualizations: We build web-based applications (shiny) and can setup automated reporting (cron).

Human Resources Analytics

Organizations are collecting volumes of data on their personnel, much of which is unstructured but very important to the business. Human resources data science can be used to better understand employee engagement, measure and forecast turnover, and measure the causal effect of HR decisions on the business. We can help with:

  • Employee Turnover Analysis: Characterizing the associated turnover risk of given populations.

  • Job Change Analysis: Investigating segments that are more/less likely to experience job changes and promotions.

  • Payroll and Labor Cost Analysis: Show the importance of Human Resources on the business.

  • Programmatic Structuring of Data: Collect, clean and manipulate data from textual files and other unstructured data sources.

  • Automated Analytics Reporting and Visualizations: Web-based applications (shiny) and automated reporting.

Manufacturing Analytics

Process industries generate enormous volumes of data, but many have failed to make use of this mountain of potential intelligence. Manufacturing data science can be used to better understand process and quality control, detect anomalies and optimize manufacturing processes. We can help with:

  • Predictive Maintenance Analysis: Build models using the historical performance data of machines to forecast when one is likely to fail, limit the time it is out of service, and identify the root cause of the problem.

  • Profit-Per-Hour Maximization: Determine the effect of large numbers of parameters on sales and provide intelligence on how best to capitalize by leveraging critical parameters.

  • Yield Energy Throughput Analysis: Optimize yield, throughput, and material costs to maximize the profitability of each process step.

  • Automated Analytics Reporting and Visualizations: Web-based applications (shiny) and automated reporting.
